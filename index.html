Introduction

Hello, I'm Cooper Robertson, a computer engineering student at Mississippi State University. With a passion for technology and an interest in exploring the intricacies of computer systems, I am excited to present my proposal for my dissection project. This project provided valuable insights into the inner workings of the TI-30X IIS calculator. This web page serves as a platform to share my discoveries and insights gained from the dissection process. Join me as I explore and discuss what I've learned about the product, uncovering its fascinating intricacies and shedding light on its underlying technology.

Lessons Learned

Throughout the dissection of the TI-30X IIS calculator, several key lessons were learned about the design and functionality of modern calculators, as well as the challenges and intricacies of hardware engineering:

  • Component Integration: The compact arrangement of circuitry showcases advanced engineering that optimizes space and enhances functionality.
  • Power Management: Understanding how the calculator manages power consumption was crucial, reflecting on energy-efficient design principles.
  • User Interface Design: The ergonomic layout of buttons and display highlights the importance of user-centered design in product development.
  • Technological Evolution: Insights into the development and evolution of calculator technology over the years, emphasizing the transition from mechanical parts to integrated circuits.

These learnings not only enhance my understanding of device design but also inspire innovative approaches to technology and product development.
