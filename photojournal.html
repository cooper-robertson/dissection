Photo Journal

Welcome to the photo journal section of my dissection project! On this page, I provide an immersive experience into the intricaties of the TI-30X IIS calculator. Through a series of captivating images, the process of dissecting and exploring the inner workings of the calculator is shown. From the initial teardown, to the detailed examination of individual components, and finally to the reassembly, each photograph and description provides insights into the innerworkings of the TI-30X IIS solar paneled calculator.

Weighing

Welcome to the weighing portion of the dissection. In this section of my photo journal, I document the weight of each individual component of the TI-30x IIS calculator. These measurements were taken in grams.

Rubber Keypad Padding
The rubber keypad weighed 5 grams.
Back Casing
The back casing weighed 27 grams.
Cover
The cover weighed 29 grams.
Keypad Key
The keys of the keypad all weighed less than a gram.
Battery
The battery weighed 2 grams.
Front Casing
The front casing weighed 25 grams.
PCB's and LCD
The two PCB boards and the LCD weighed 29 grams. (They were all attached via ribbons that could not be removed.)


Reassembly

Welcome to the reassembly portion of the dissection. In this section of my photo journal, I document the reassembly and post-reassembaly successful functionality of the TI-30x IIS calculator.

PCB and LCD Reassembly
Here is a photo of the reassembly of the two PCB boards and and the LCD display.The bottom PCB was staked down with plastic rivets that had to be removed during dissassembly. Thus, hot glue was utilized to glue down the bottom PCB.
Back Reassembly
The back casing clipped back in perfectly. There were four screw holes that needed to be screwed in as well.
Reassembly Functionality
The calculator was successfully functioning post-reassembly.
